1 · What it is, who it's for

A real-time training weather display for Fort Rucker pilots.

My Weather Pal is a single-pane briefing-room display that consolidates every piece of weather information a Fort Rucker training pilot needs into one glanceable view. It replaces the workflow of opening four browser tabs (army.mil PDFs, aviationweather.gov METARs, FAA WAAS map, NOAA TAF text) with a single URL that auto-refreshes every five minutes.

Who uses it

  • Pilots — quick GO/NO-GO check before stepping to the aircraft
  • Instructors — visual aid during pre-flight briefings; everyone in the room reads the same data
  • Standardization & Operations — at-a-glance situational awareness during the duty day
  • Schedulers / SDOs — early visibility into deteriorating conditions that may affect periods later in the day

What it answers, instantly

The four questions it's optimized for
  1. Is Cairns AAF GO or NO-GO for VFR training right now?
  2. Are the four stagefields (Allen, Toth, Highbluff, Lucas) trainable, and if not, when will they be?
  3. Can students fly solo today (1200/3, ≤15 kt wind, ≤10 kt gust spread)?
  4. If we have to launch IFR, do we meet taxi mins, do we have legal alternates, and what does the TAF say for the next 12 hours?

Why it exists

The official Cairns Weather products (Mission Execution Forecast and DD Form 175-1) are issued as PDFs three times a day. They contain everything a pilot needs but require reading dense regulatory format and cross-referencing live METAR/TAF data from separate sources. My Weather Pal does that cross-reference automatically and presents the result as a HUD-style training-focused dashboard.

The tool encodes USAACE Regulation 115-1 (4 March 2024) — every threshold, zone definition, and amendment criterion comes from the regulation. It is a pilot reference, not a substitute for official forecaster briefings.

How the pipeline works

End-to-end flow
An always-on Mac mini in the office runs a weather-pal-publisher CLI (Swift) every 5 minutes. The CLI scrapes army.mil for new PDFs, parses them using PDFKit + custom regex (with Vision OCR fallback), fetches live METARs/TAFs/WAAS, and pushes a single data.json file to a public GitHub repository. The dashboard, hosted on GitHub Pages, fetches that JSON every 5 minutes and re-renders. All third-party fetching happens server-side, so the browser only ever talks to GitHub.

Trust and verification

The dashboard always tells you how fresh each piece of data is. You'll see:

  • Fetched X min ago — when this browser last pulled data.json
  • Published X min ago — when the publishing server last pushed new data (publisher runs every 5 min). Colored heartbeat dot: green <10 min, yellow 10–20 min, red >20 min
  • OLD WEATHER banner — appears if the displayed forecast is from a previous calendar day or its valid period has expired
  • USING FORECAST · METAR IS STALE badge — if the live METAR is >80 min old, the dashboard synthesizes conditions from the GOLDFISH area forecast and flags the substitution prominently

The design principle: stale data is always labeled, never silently shown as fresh.

4 · VFR Operations

The single most-used part of the dashboard.

Training Status panels

Five glowing tactical-display panels. Each shows:

  • Top color stripe — flight category color (VFR / MVFR / IFR / LIFR or NO GO)
  • Station name in tracked Rajdhani caps
  • Big status text: VFR GO · NO GO · UNKNOWN
  • Subtitle: ceiling/vis or area + countdown ("GOLDFISH · Trainable in 2h 15m")
  • Recommended landing direction (VFR panels) — runway with the most headwind, plus crosswind side
  • SOLO badge (stagefields, AM/PM only) — SOLO GO or NO SOLO with reason
  • Advisory pip — small pulsing ⚠️ corner badge if an active advisory affects that area

Cairns AAFCeiling ≥ 1000 ft AND visibility ≥ 3 SM

Bottom row of the Cairns panel. Three independent tier dots showing whether current KOZR conditions support a Special VFR recovery:

Multiple green tiers means recovery has a buffer. Single green tier means you're operating right at the edge. Zero greens turns the row red.

Area Forecasts

Each card shows that zone's forecast text, color-coded by flight category per time segment. Time-aware: the active segment based on current Zulu time drives the GO/NO-GO badge and trainability countdown shown below the area name.

Trainability labels:

  • ✅ Weather is Trainable — current segment meets 700/1
  • 🕐 Trainable in Xh Xm — currently NO-GO, future segment meets mins
  • ⚠️ Untrainable in Xh Xm — currently GO, future segment drops below
  • ❌ Weather is Untrainable — below mins, no future improvement in this period

Aerodrome Forecasts (TAF-style)

3-column grid of FROM/TEMPO/PROB blocks for the operating area's airfields, parsed from the DD 175-1's terminal-forecast section. Each card:

  • ICAO header colored by the first non-TEMPO period's flight category
  • Embedded current METAR directly below the ICAO (small monospace, colored by FAA category, green-tinted background when current, red when stale)
  • FROM / predominant periods: header + weather on separate lines, colored by category
  • TEMPO periods: gray, indented under the FROM they modify
  • "Same as KOZR:" sister-airfield list when multiple fields share a forecast

7 · Special features

The non-obvious tools tucked into the dashboard.

7-Day Outlook (VFR banner center)

Compact dropdown with 7 day-letters, each carrying a single colored dot summarizing the worst category across that day's AM/PM/N1 periods. Click to expand a detailed 14-period × 7-category grid plus the forecaster's synopsis. Driven by the 06-12-mar-26.pdf-style outlook chart issued weekly by Cairns Weather.

KOZR TAF strip (VFR banner)

Six colored hourly cells for the next 6 hours of KOZR TAF, expandable to a 12-hour view. Each cell's color is the FAA flight category of the non-TEMPO/non-PROB TAF group in effect at that hour. Falls back to a "RAW TAF" text view when aviationweather.gov has no TAF cached and the parser doesn't have enough structure to color individual hours.

WAAS / GPS Coverage (IFR banner center)

Real-time FAA WAAS coverage classification for KOZR. The publisher fetches the official FAA snapshot, samples a 17×17 pixel region around Cairns, drops near-white state-border pixels, averages the rest, and threshold-matches against the FAA color ramp. Result is one of:

  • Full LPV200 — best precision approach available (special blue dot + glow)
  • LPV — full precision approach
  • LNAV/VNAV only
  • Marginal
  • Degraded
  • Unknown — sampling failed

Publish Log modal

📋 Publish Log button opens a modal with the recent publish history embedded in data.json: 6 stat cards (Last Run · Today's runs/failures · Success Rate · Streak · Failures · Avg Duration) plus a scrolling list of entries (newest first, green border for success, red for failure). Useful when troubleshooting why the "Published" chip is climbing into yellow/red.

ADS-B + Windy maps (bottom row)

Two side-by-side iframes, both collapsible:

8 · Status & failure indicators

How to recognize when something's wrong.

The dashboard never silently shows stale data. Every failure mode has a visible indicator. Here's how to read them:

Heartbeat pip turns red and pulses

What it means
The publishing server (Mac mini in the office) hasn't pushed new data in > 40 minutes. Either the server is offline, the publisher process crashed, GitHub auth expired, or the network is down.

What to do: SSH to the publishing host, check the publisher log, verify the launchd agents are loaded. Until restored, the dashboard will keep showing whatever was last published — visibly stale via the pip + age counter.

OLD WEATHER banner (yellow)

What it means
The currently displayed forecast is operationally stale — either issued on a previous calendar day in the operator's local time, or its valid period has already ended (e.g., the N1 brief for last night still showing because today's brief hasn't been issued yet). Yellow 3px viewport border + diagonal striping over forecast-driven sections.

What to do: Treat it as last-known. The METAR / Cairns / IFR Training / Alternate Planning sections still read live data and aren't dimmed.

USING FORECAST · METAR IS STALE (cyan badge)

What it means
The live KOZR METAR is > 80 minutes old. The dashboard has switched to synthesizing conditions from the GOLDFISH area-forecast segment (because Cairns AAF sits inside the GOLDFISH training area). The pseudo-METAR is prefixed FCST so it's obviously not a real observation.

What to do: Auto-reverts as soon as a fresh METAR arrives. No manual action needed.

UNKNOWN status (yellow ❓)

What it means
Cairns shows UNKNOWN when the live METAR is missing entirely AND no GOLDFISH fallback is available either (rare). Stagefields don't go UNKNOWN — they always evaluate against the area forecast which is part of the brief itself.

Active Advisories card glows amber

What it means
At least one AWA/TWA/WATCH/WARNING is currently in effect. Multiple stack vertically inside the card; each shows expiry countdown ("Expires in 1h 24m"). An "Upcoming" banner above the active list previews the next pending advisory (only the soonest is shown).

Old Cairns Weather data > 24 hours

Day-after-day (e.g., a holiday weekend) the OLD WEATHER banner persists across calendar boundaries. The publisher will keep retrying army.mil every 5 minutes, silently — once a new brief lands the banner clears automatically.

9 · TV / kiosk mode

For the briefing-room display.

Append ?tv=1 to the URL to enable kiosk display. Designed for distance viewing on a 4K TV.

What changes in TV mode

  • Base font 15px → 20px
  • Training status text 26px → 38px
  • All card elements scaled up 25–50%
  • Cursor auto-hides after 3 seconds of inactivity
  • Page automatically reloads at 03:00 local to prevent browser memory bloat
  • Auto-detects when the source HTML changes (server deploy) and reloads silently

Auto-refresh behaviors

TriggerWhat happens
Every 5 minutesRe-fetch data.json
Every minuteRe-render TAF strip and re-evaluate auto-period (AM → PM → N1 transitions)
Every 30 secondsUpdate "Fetched X min ago" / "Published X min ago" labels
03:00 localFull page reload (memory hygiene)
HTML deploy detectedSilent reload (compares ETag to initial)

Setup recommendation

Suggested briefing-room setup
  • Modern 4K TV (40+ inches), wired to Ethernet or strong WiFi
  • Connected device: small form-factor PC (NUC), Mac mini, or Apple TV with browser app
  • Browser: Chrome or Safari, full-screen mode (F11 or Cmd+Ctrl+F)
  • URL: https://mrwhistletips.github.io/weather-pal-dashboard/?tv=1
  • Power: TV always-on during operating hours; the daily 03:00 reload prevents memory leaks
